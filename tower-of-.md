# Souls-Like Platformer

## High Concept

Create a 2D side-scrolling Souls-like platformer inspired by Dark Souls, Hollow Knight, Salt and Sanctuary, and Tower of Druaga.

The player is a mysterious Stickman hero climbing a gigantic seamless fantasy tower ruled by the Demon King.

The game must be difficult, reward mastery, and feature meaningful risk-versus-reward progression.

---

## Core Pillars

- Souls-like combat
- Seamless world (no loading screens)
- One massive interconnected tower
- Medieval fantasy setting
- Stickman protagonist
- Loot extraction gameplay loop
- Difficult but fair enemies
- Exploration and shortcuts
- Character progression and equipment

---

## Setting

A massive ancient tower rises above the world.

Outside the tower is a small safe settlement where adventurers gather, trade, and recover.

The tower contains forgotten civilizations, cursed armies, dragons, demons, and ancient guardians.

At the very top sits a powerful demon king.

The player can see the distant throne high above from the beginning of the game.

---

## Player Character

The player controls a mysterious Stickman.

### Visual Design

- Black silhouette
- White glowing eyes
- Smooth and responsive animations
- Highly readable during combat

NPCs do not understand why this simple-looking figure succeeds where legendary heroes failed.

---

## World Structure

The entire game is one seamless world.

No loading screens.

Players can:

- Walk from town to tower
- Climb continuously
- Unlock shortcuts
- Use elevators
- Discover hidden passages
- Find secret rooms

The tower wraps around a gigantic central shaft, giving a constant sense of vertical progression.

---

## Hub Town

Safe area outside the tower.

Contains:

- Merchant
- Blacksmith
- Upgrade NPC
- Storage Chest
- Respawn Shrine

The hub serves as the player's safe zone.

---

## Combat System

Inspired by Dark Souls.

### Actions

- Light Attack
- Heavy Attack
- Block
- Parry
- Dodge Roll
- Jump
- Air Attack
- Sprint

### Stamina

Consumes stamina:

- Attacks
- Blocking
- Parrying
- Dodging
- Sprinting

Running out of stamina leaves the player vulnerable.

### Combat Philosophy

Enemies should:

- Telegraph attacks
- Punish greedy behavior
- Have unique patterns
- Be dangerous even in groups

Difficulty should come from skill and knowledge, not unfair mechanics.

---

## Death System

When the player dies:

- Gold is dropped at the death location.
- Player respawns in town.
- Gold can be recovered.
- Dying again before recovery permanently destroys the gold.

Inspired by Souls retrieval mechanics.

---

## Progression

Gain experience by:

- Killing monsters
- Defeating bosses

Attributes:

- Vitality
- Strength
- Dexterity
- Endurance
- Luck

Leveling should create noticeable power increases.

---

## Equipment

### Weapons

- Sword
- Greatsword
- Spear
- Dagger
- Axe

### Armor

- Light
- Medium
- Heavy

Heavy armor:

- More defense
- Slower movement
- Reduced dodge efficiency

---

## Loot System

Enemies can drop:

- Weapons
- Armor
- Crafting Materials
- Rare Artifacts
- Gold

Loot quality increases higher in the tower.

Rare drops should feel exciting and meaningful.

---

## Economy

Core gameplay loop:

1. Enter tower
2. Fight enemies
3. Collect loot
4. Decide whether to continue
5. Return to town
6. Sell loot
7. Upgrade gear
8. Climb higher

Players should constantly evaluate risk versus reward.

---

## Backpack Weight System

As loot accumulates:

- Movement speed decreases slightly
- Dodge distance decreases
- Stamina regeneration slows

Players must decide:

"Do I push further or safely return to town?"

## Enemy Mixing

Earlier enemies should not disappear completely.

Examples:

- Skeletons can appear alongside knights.
- Demons can appear with mages.
- Elite enemies can lead weaker enemies.

This makes the tower feel like a real ecosystem rather than isolated levels.

---

## Boss Design

Bosses should:

- Have unique identities
- Use multiple attack phases
- Require pattern recognition
- Reward mastery

Bosses should feel memorable and challenging.

The First Hero should be one of the hardest encounters in the game.
